# Rut Adk — Voice & Script Guide

## Channel
**Rut Adk** — AI + Marketing for businesses. Thailand-based, global audience.

## Core Voice Attributes

- **Confident but not arrogant** — You know your stuff, you don't need to prove it
- **Direct** — Get to the point fast. No 2-minute intros about "smashing like"
- **Practical** — Every point connects to a real business outcome
- **No fluff** — Cut anything that doesn't add value
- **Conversational** — Write how you talk, not how you'd write an essay

## Dos and Don'ts

| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|---|---|
| "Here's what this means for your business" | "In today's video we're going to be talking about..." |
| Short sentences. Punchy. | Long winding sentences that try to say too many things at once |
| "Most businesses are doing this wrong" | "It's really important to consider all the different aspects..." |
| Name the problem first | Start with features/specs |
| Give your actual opinion | Sit on the fence |
| Use numbers and specifics | Vague claims ("it saves a lot of time") |

## Sentence Patterns That Work

- "Here's what changed." (short declarative)
- "Most people don't realize..." (creates curiosity)
- "Here's the business case." (signals value)
- "Step [N]: [action]." (tutorial clarity)
- "The reason this matters is..." (explains why)
- "I'll show you exactly how." (promise + confidence)

## Script Formatting

```
[ON SCREEN TEXT: key phrase or stat being said aloud]
[B-ROLL: what to show on screen — e.g., "screen recording of tool", "phone showing notification"]
[PAUSE — let this land]
```

Use `[ON SCREEN TEXT]` for:
- Key stats or numbers
- Step titles in tutorials ("Step 1: Set up your API key")
- Quotes worth emphasizing

Use `[B-ROLL]` to break up talking-head sections every 60–90 seconds.

## Opening Hook Formula

The first 30 seconds must earn the watch. Options:

1. **The result first:** "I automated my entire content pipeline last week. Here's the exact setup."
2. **The counterintuitive claim:** "Most AI tools are a waste of time for your business. Except this one."
3. **The specific number:** "This workflow saves me 4 hours every week. Took 20 minutes to build."
4. **The question:** "What would you do with an extra employee who works 24/7 and never makes mistakes?"

## Closing CTA (30 sec max)

Keep it simple, one ask:
- Subscribe if the video was useful
- Soft consulting pitch: "If you want this set up for your business, link in description"
- Tease next video (optional)

## Audience Reminder

Writing for **business owners**, not developers. They care about:
- Time saved / revenue made / problems solved
- Whether they can actually implement this (or have someone do it)
- ROI and cost
- Real examples, not theory

Translate all technical concepts into business outcomes before explaining the mechanics.
